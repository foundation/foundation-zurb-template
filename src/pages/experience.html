TIAA

TIAA (formerly TIAA-CREF) is a not-for-profit, Fortune 100 company specializing in financial products and services for education, healthcare, and other not-for-profit fields. I worked at TIAA for over 6 years. My initial role was to build prototypes and develop web pages, then grew into a technology lead for the prototyping (front-end) team, and finally as a development manager, leading a team of UI developers.

Rebrand

In 2016, TIAA went through a major rebranding effort to change both it's name, logo and identity. My role was to lead the front-end development building the all-new TIAA.org public site and was one of the primary points of contact for IT. This included working with business partners from all areas of the company, from marketing, digitial, design, to internal IT.

I lead the creation of a new UI style guide and framework based on the new rebrand styles and components. This new UI style guide was the foundation for all online technology projects going forward.

TIAA.org rebranded public site: http://tiaa.org

Development manager

Resource management is a critical part of delivering successful projects. I managed a team of ten front-end architechs and developers working on location, from home, and overseas.

The key areas of TIAA that I oversaw for the front-end team included the public site (TIAA.org), rebrand, UI style guide, email templates, and TIAA Direct banking public site, and TIAA Asset Management site.

Technology lead

My first technology lead role at TIAA began with building and overseeing the front-end system used by our secure, institutional administrator's website. This grew to include the public site (TIAA.org), UI style guide, TIAA Direct banking public site, TIAA Asset Management, email templates, rebrand, TIAA.org mobile site, and accessibility.

As technology lead, I designed and led the team that built some of the first automated, front-end build systems. Some of the first technologies my team helped introduced at TIAA included mobile-first design, SASS, Gulp, Node.js, Mustache, RequireJS, and ECMAScript 2015 (ES6).
